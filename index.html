KINDERSPARK

Kinderspark was an Innovation programme which lasted 6 months and was hosted by Mahindras and Think Young.

We worked in teams and collaborated to solve problems related to the auto and farm industries. We chose the problem of making shared mobility vehicles safe during the pandemic and explored innovative solutions. We proposed the idea of introducing safety features like smart barriers and hands-free (automatic) doors. We also made digital prototypes of our safe cars to demonstrate to stakeholders. Ultimately, we pitched our final solution to a panel of experts from industry and academia. I learnt a lot in this programme including design thinking and innovative problem solving.

GRIP

We spent the first 5 months researching problems and coming up with our business plan and prototype. We covered everything from talking to government school students and understanding their situation to developing working prototypes to show them and receive feedback.
We spend the final week in NUS, finalising our ideas and pitching them to a jury of professors from the institution.

We built an app and website for our idea- EduSphereX. It was an all-in-one platform for students of all backgrounds that covered their academic and social needs through its social media platform that better connected peers with both each other and the school and informed them about opportunities relating to their passions and interests. It provided notes and lectures on individual topics in subjects and even had VR experiences for certain concepts fully immerse the students in their learning. All the content was available in local languages to increase accessibility, especially among students from government schools. to It also promoted holistic learning and community action by providing a list of community service opportunities for students to sign up for and directly connecting them with the people in charge.
